Manufacturing Programmable Matter

Our Mission

We are reimagining fiber spinning by combining the manufacturing lessons of biology with new forms of matter, creating fibers that neither nature nor industry have been able to make. We are building a universal fiber fabricator: that leverages advances in protein design, soft-matter physics, microfluidics, and fiber spinning. We beleive that changing how we make our materials can fundamentally transform billion-dollar industries.

Why it Matters

Nature has evolved fibers that are strong, flexible, self-assembling, and encoded with dense structural information rivaling our own microchips. Industry has tried to catch up using petroleum chemistry and extrusion, producing abundance but also brittleness: global supply chains that snap under stress, capital equipment that can't adapt, and switiching costs so high that new materials never escape the lab. Reimagining fiber spinning lets us borrow nature's elegance without being constrained by it, unlocking materials that are high-performance without the usual trade-offs. Our technology is aimed at enabling the next generation of fiber technologies, from new actuators for highly dextrous robots, to gradient fibers for biomedical tissue replacement, to computational fibers with capabilities that push beyond today's materials and into entirely new domains.
